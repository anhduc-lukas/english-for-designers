# Real Stories: Three Projects That Shaped How I Design

*Case Study — English for Designers, 2026*

**Year:** 2025–2026
**Role:** Creative Director, Popcorn Group
**Focus:** Brand identity, visual campaigns, dark UI design

---

## Why These Three Projects

I don't pick projects for the portfolio. I pick them because something real was at stake.

A catering startup that needed to stand out in a crowded market. A city that needed its citizens to care about cleanliness. A consulting brand that needed to feel premium — without feeling cold.

Three different problems. One approach: design that tells a real story.

---

## Project 1 — FUNGĒ Catering: Making Food Feel Like a Brand

The brief was simple: fun, modern, Asian-inspired food brand for a catering company.

The challenge was less simple. *Fun* without being cheap. *Asian-inspired* without being a cliché. *Modern* without losing warmth.

I developed the full visual identity — logo, color system, packaging direction, and social media visual language.

The result was a bold red and cream palette, a custom wordmark with attitude, and a visual system that works equally well on a business card and a catering box.

> The goal wasn't to make it look good. It was to make it feel like something you'd trust with your event.

---

## Project 2 — #pročistšímost: Design for a City That Stopped Caring

Most city campaigns fail because they lecture. *Don't litter. Respect public space.* Nobody listens.

The brief for Most was different: positive motivation. Not guilt. Pride.

I developed the key visual concept, campaign layouts, and outdoor formats. The visual language used bold Czech copy — direct, almost confrontational — paired with a high-contrast green and blue palette that felt civic but not bureaucratic.

*"Čistota začíná tady."* Cleanliness starts here. Not a complaint. A statement.

The campaign ran on bins, billboards, and digital formats across the city.

**What I learned:** Tone of voice is a design decision. The same message in the wrong voice fails. We chose confidence over shame — and it worked.

---

## Project 3 — Basti Brand: When Dark UI Has to Work Hard

Basti Brand is a consulting partner for gastronomy and franchise businesses. The product is serious: business strategy, menu engineering, franchising toolkits.

The visual direction needed to match: dark, premium, structured.

I designed the full web UI — layout, visual hierarchy, component system. The challenge was readability. Dark interfaces are easy to make look impressive. Hard to make actually usable.

Key decisions:
- Green accent on near-black background for high contrast
- Large metric callouts (+18–32% revenue growth) as anchor points
- Structured process section to build trust through clarity

The footer carries: *"Provozováno s láskou od Popcorn Group."*

> Clarity is a form of respect. If someone can't find what they need in 10 seconds, the design failed — no matter how good it looks.

---

## What These Projects Have in Common

I like mixing branding, campaigns, UI, and photography. Not because I can't choose — but because real projects rarely fit one box.

What connects them:

- **Clarity over decoration.** Every project has one main message. Design should make it louder, not more complicated.
- **Real people, real stakes.** A catering owner, a city, a business consultant. Not mood boards.
- **Visual voice.** I'm still learning — but I already know what I want my work to sound like.
