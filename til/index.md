# Your Filename Is Also a Design Decision

*Today I Learned — English for Designers, February 2026*

---

## The Moment

I was organizing a client project folder.

Hundreds of files. Names like `final_v3_REAL_USE THIS.jpg`.

And I realized — I had been treating filenames as a technical detail.

Not as design.

---

## The Learning

A filename is the first thing someone sees before they open anything.

It sets expectations. It builds trust. Or it destroys it.

`maryia-outdoor-portrait.jpg` tells a story.

`518250484-c0ba6c99.jpeg` tells nothing.

Same principle as alt text. Same principle as a headline.

**Every word you choose is a design decision.**

---

## Why It Matters

I run a studio. I send files to clients every week.

A well-named file says: *I thought about you.*

A poorly named file says: *I thought about me.*

That's the difference between a deliverable and an experience.

---

## Takeaway

Writing is designing.

Not just in big things — manifestos, case studies, campaign copy.

In small things too.

Your filename. Your folder structure. Your commit message.

**Design every detail of the experience.**
